# Gradient Derivation Methodology

## How Gradients Are Computed

Gradients represent the directional pull of patterns toward specific research actions.

### Formula (Conceptual)

```
gradient = weighted_sum(pattern_signals) × confidence × novelty
```

### Signal Weights

- **Frequency**: How often a pattern appears
- **Impact**: How much it affects outcomes
- **Novelty**: How new/uncommon the pattern is
- **Correlation**: Strength of association with success/failure

### Gradient Clustering

Related gradients are grouped into clusters. Each cluster represents a research direction.

Clusters are scored by:
- Aggregate gradient magnitude
- Internal coherence
- Actionability (can we do something about it?)
