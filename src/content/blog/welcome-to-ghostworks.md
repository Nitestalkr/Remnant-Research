Ghostworks is now live — a research and deployment surface for two interconnected frameworks: a drive-based cognitive architecture (GNW) and a gradient-driven research optimization loop (GRAO).

## What exists today

The site documents both frameworks at the same specification depth we use internally:

- **GNW** — a five-drive cognitive architecture with a twelve-step cognitive cycle, boredom-based self-initiation, and a formal stability test battery (five tests, all passing).
- **GRAO** — a self-improving research loop that treats research direction as gradient descent over execution traces. Round 39 detected policy saturation at 92.7% success ratio.

Full pseudocode, parameter values, and cycle data are published alongside the site at the [research repository](https://github.com/Nitestalkr/Remnant-Research).

## Why publish openly

Cognitive architectures like GNWT, ACT-R, and SOAR have existed for decades, but most remain descriptive rather than operational. The translation gap between theory and running code is where most work stops.

Ghostworks closes that gap publicly. Every framework here includes:

- Full parameter values and calibrated thresholds
- Stability tests with measurable results
- Live cycle data from a system actually running
- Pseudocode structured enough to be reproduced

## What's next

GNW is moving through Phase 6 — cross-agent coordination, drive score broadcast, weight sharing, and multi-agent synchronization. GRAO is entering its exploration phase after detecting policy saturation.

This blog will publish deeper breakdowns of specific mechanisms, stability results, and architectural decisions as they stabilize.
